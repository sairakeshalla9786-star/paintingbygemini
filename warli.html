Warli Paintings (Maharashtra Tribal Expressions)

Dating back to 2500 BCE, Warli art is a minimalist expression created by the indigenous tribes of the Western Ghats. Painted on canvas or mud walls coated with cow dung and red ochre (Geru), it uses white paste made from rice flour flour mixtures to form distinct geometric shapes—circles, triangles, and lines—representing harmony with nature.
